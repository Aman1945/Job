# INTERNAL AUDIT PROCEDURE
**NexusOMS Enterprise - ISO 9001:2015 Clause 9.2 | ISO 27001:2022 Clause 9.2**  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Effective Date**: February 12, 2026

---

## 1. PURPOSE

Establish a systematic approach for conducting internal audits to verify compliance with ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 27001:2022 requirements and identify improvement opportunities.

---

## 2. SCOPE

Internal audits cover:
- Quality Management System (ISO 9001)
- Information Security Management System (ISO 27001)
- All departments and processes
- Compliance with policies and procedures

---

## 3. AUDIT TYPES

### 3.1 System Audit
- Full QMS/ISMS audit
- Frequency: Annually
- Duration: 5-7 days
- Scope: All clauses of ISO 9001 and ISO 27001

### 3.2 Process Audit
- Specific process evaluation
- Frequency: Quarterly
- Duration: 1-2 days
- Scope: Selected processes (order management, logistics, etc.)

### 3.3 Compliance Audit
- Regulatory compliance check
- Frequency: As needed
- Duration: 1 day
- Scope: GDPR, IT Act, etc.

---

## 4. AUDIT TEAM

### 4.1 Roles
- **Lead Auditor**: Plans and conducts audit, prepares report
- **Auditor**: Assists in audit execution
- **Auditee**: Department/process being audited
- **Management Representative**: Coordinates audit activities

### 4.2 Competence Requirements
- ISO 9001/27001 training
- Auditing skills training
- Knowledge of audited processes
- Independence (cannot audit own work)

---

## 5. AUDIT PROCESS

### PHASE 1: Planning (Week 1-2)

**Step 1: Define Audit Scope**
```
Audit ID: IA-YYYY-MM-XXX
Audit Type: System / Process / Compliance
Standard: ISO 9001 / ISO 27001 / Both
Departments: _______________
Processes: _______________
Audit Dates: _______________
```

**Step 2: Assign Audit Team**
- Lead Auditor: _______________
- Auditors: _______________
- Observers: _______________

**Step 3: Prepare Audit Plan**
```
Date | Time | Process/Department | Auditor | Auditee
-----|------|-------------------|---------|--------
     |      |                   |         |
```

**Step 4: Notify Auditees**
- Send audit plan 2 weeks in advance
- Request necessary documents
- Schedule interviews

---

### PHASE 2: Preparation (Week 3)

**Step 5: Document Review**
- Review policies and procedures
- Review previous audit reports
- Review non-conformities and corrective actions
- Prepare audit checklist

**Audit Checklist Template**:
```
Clause | Requirement | Evidence | Finding | Notes
-------|-------------|----------|---------|------
       |             |          |         |
```

---

### PHASE 3: Execution (Week 4)

**Step 6: Opening Meeting**
- Introduce audit team
- Confirm audit scope and schedule
- Explain audit process
- Address questions

**Step 7: Conduct Audit**
- Interview personnel
- Review documents and records
- Observe processes
- Collect evidence
- Document findings

**Interview Questions (Sample)**:
- "Can you explain this process?"
- "How do you ensure quality?"
- "What do you do if there's a problem?"
- "Where is this documented?"
- "Can you show me an example?"

**Step 8: Document Findings**

**Non-Conformity Report**:
```
NC ID: NC-IA-YYYY-MM-XXX
Clause: _______________
Type: Major / Minor / Observation
Description: _______________
Evidence: _______________
Root Cause: _______________
```

**Step 9: Closing Meeting**
- Present findings
- Discuss non-conformities
- Agree on corrective actions
- Set follow-up dates

---

### PHASE 4: Reporting (Week 5)

**Step 10: Prepare Audit Report**

**Report Structure**:
1. Executive Summary
2. Audit Scope and Objectives
3. Audit Team
4. Audit Methodology
5. Findings Summary
   - Conformities
   - Non-Conformities (Major/Minor)
   - Observations
   - Opportunities for Improvement
6. Detailed Findings
7. Conclusions
8. Recommendations

**Step 11: Distribute Report**
- Send to auditees within 5 days
- Send to management
- File in audit records

---

### PHASE 5: Follow-up (Week 6-10)

**Step 12: Corrective Action Plan**
```
NC ID: _______________
Corrective Action: _______________
Responsible: _______________
Deadline: _______________
Status: Open / In Progress / Closed
```

**Step 13: Verify Corrective Actions**
- Review evidence of implementation
- Conduct follow-up audit (if needed)
- Close non-conformities

---

## 7. FINDING CLASSIFICATION

### 7.1 Major Non-Conformity
- Absence of a required system element
- Systemic failure
- Significant risk to quality/security
- **Action**: Immediate corrective action required

### 7.2 Minor Non-Conformity
- Isolated incident
- Partial implementation
- Low risk
- **Action**: Corrective action within 30 days

### 7.3 Observation
- Potential for improvement
- Not a non-conformity yet
- **Action**: Consider for improvement

### 7.4 Opportunity for Improvement (OFI)
- Best practice suggestion
- Enhancement opportunity
- **Action**: Optional implementation

## 9. AUDIT RECORDS

Maintain for each audit:
- Audit plan
- Audit checklist
- Interview notes
- Evidence collected
- Non-conformity reports
- Audit report
- Corrective action records

**Retention**: 3 years

---

## 10. AUDIT METRICS

Track and report:
- Number of audits conducted
- Number of non-conformities (Major/Minor)
- Corrective action closure rate
- Average time to close NCs
- Repeat non-conformities
- Audit effectiveness
